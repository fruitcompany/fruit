Graduation Path Analysis Scheduler (GPAS)

 

This is a tutorial only. No log in is required.

Introduction

Graduation Path Analysis Scheduler lets undergraduates in choosing courses and following the right path to graduation in their majors. It works side-by-side with the interactive Degree Progress Report (DPR). Always use a DPR and GPAS in consultation with your academic advisor.

  Step 1

Log in to GPAS at www.gpas.edu with your username and password (Left). For the first time users, use your CSUN User ID and Password, CSUN Email address and Student ID to create an account (right). Be careful while providing this information. You will be logged in automatically after creating a new account.

 

 

 

  Step 2

Your GPAS page displays. Click on the (+) icon to Add Path.

 

 

 

  Step 3

Select a major and type the information of when you enrolled at CSUN. They will generate a graduation path for your selected major.

 

 

  Step 4

An entire path to graduation is created and saved to your account. The blue buttons represent the courses which are compulsory in order to graduate in your major. The brown buttons represent particularly the GE courses, which you can select from a list of courses.

 

 

 

  This navigation pane contains the information of your major, number of units you completed, and your estimated graduating semester. You can download this plan in your computer by clicking on the folder icon.

Using the message icon, you can see the general information about the courses listed on your graduation path.

 

Step 5

You can create and save multiple graduation plans by generating searches for the same major or different major. You can keep our recommended graduation plan as it is, and generate a new plan (using the Step 3), which you want to customize slightly to fit with your own time-schedule. Our graduation plan is generalized. Everybody has different issues and so we allow making personal graduation plan.

 

  You can drag between the courses of two different semesters to make a schedule of your own choice. However, if a course has a prerequisite and is added or dragged to a location where the requirements have not been met, the user will get an illegal move notification.

 

  Classes planned in the future where availability status is unknown will show yellow notification.

 

NOTE: If the GPAS does not run successfully and generates an error message, submit a GPAS Review Request to explain the problem. You may also submit a GPAS Review Request for other specific questions. Terms and Conditions to Use
